## The problem

Checkout abandonment was running at 61% — well above industry average. Session recordings showed users dropping off at the payment step, not because of intent, but because of friction: too many fields, unclear error states, and a three-page flow that felt longer than it was.

## What I did

I ran a discovery sprint with the payments team and design: five days of session analysis, exit survey synthesis, and competitive benchmarking. We identified three root causes — field overload on the address step, ambiguous card error messages, and no progress indicator.

I wrote a spec that proposed collapsing the three-page flow into a single scrollable page with an inline progress indicator. The key design decision was removing optional fields from the critical path entirely and surfacing them post-purchase.

## Outcome

- **34% reduction** in checkout abandonment over 30 days
- Payment error rates dropped 18% after we rewrote error copy
- Mobile conversion improved 22% — the biggest single-page win was on smaller screens
