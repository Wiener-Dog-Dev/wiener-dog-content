## The problem

Pricing was set manually by category managers and updated in batch jobs overnight. In a market where competitor prices changed multiple times a day, we were always reacting rather than competing in real time.

## What I did

This was the most technically complex project I've scoped. I embedded with the backend team for two weeks before writing a single line of the spec — I needed to understand the constraints before proposing a solution.

The core architecture decision was whether to use a rules engine or an ML model for price recommendations. I pushed for rules first: faster to build, easier to audit, and sufficient for 80% of our SKUs. ML would come in Phase 2 for the long tail.

I wrote the technical spec covering the event stream design (Kafka), the pricing service interface, and the rollback strategy. The key product decision was to make the engine advisory by default — category managers received recommendations with confidence scores but retained override control for the first 90 days.

## Outcome

- **2M events/day** processed at p99 < 50ms
- Margin per order improved 3.1% in the first quarter
- Zero pricing incidents in the first 6 months (the advisory mode paid off — it caught three edge cases before they became live errors)
